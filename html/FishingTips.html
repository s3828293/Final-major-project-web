  • Overcast Days = Big Opportunities:
    Cloudy skies mean reduced light penetration, making fish feel safer and more likely to roam away from structure.
    Great time to fish shallower waters or target species like redfin and trout near the edges.
  • Wind Direction Matters:
    A gentle breeze blowing into the shore stirs up food and attracts baitfish‚Äîpredatory fish often follow.
    Cast with the wind at your back when possible for better lure distance and accuracy.
  • Avoid Extreme Heat or Cold Snaps:
    On hot summer afternoons, fish may go deeper seeking cooler water‚Äîtry bottom fishing in deeper lakes like Sugarloaf or Devilbend.
    Cold fronts can cause fish to become lethargic‚Äîuse slower retrieves or natural baits.
  • Morning & Evening Are Prime Time:
    Especially in summer, early morning (sunrise) and late afternoon (sunset) are peak periods for activity.
    In winter, wait until mid-morning when the water slightly warms up to increase chances.
  • Watch for Sudden Weather Changes:
    Melbourne weather is famously unpredictable‚Äîpack a light rain jacket and check radar updates before heading out.
    Sudden drops in temperature or unexpected rain can both shut down and spark short feeding windows‚Äîstay flexible.
